PanoGrounder: Bridging 2D and 3D with Panoramic Scene Representations for VLM-based 3D Visual Grounding

Author One1, Author Two1, Author Three2, Author Four1, Author Five1,2
1Affiliation A, 2Affiliation B
Paper arXiv Code

PanoGrounder localizes referred objects in 3D scenes by leveraging panoramic renderings as a 2D–3D interface.

Abstract

3D Visual Grounding (3DVG) is a critical bridge from vision-language perception to robotics, requiring both language understanding and 3D scene reasoning. Traditional supervised models leverage explicit 3D geometry but exhibit limited generalization, owing to the scarcity of 3D vision-language datasets and the limited reasoning capabilities compared to modern vision-language models (VLMs).

We propose a generalizable 3DVG framework, PanoGrounder, that couples a multi-modal panoramic representation with pretrained 2D VLMs for strong vision-language reasoning. Panoramic renderings, augmented with 3D semantic and geometric features, serve as an intermediate representation between 2D and 3D, and offer two major benefits: (i) they can be directly fed to VLMs with minimal adaptation and (ii) they retain long-range object-to-object relations thanks to their 360-degree field of view. We devise a three-stage pipeline that places a compact set of panoramic viewpoints considering the scene layout and geometry, grounds a text query on each panoramic rendering with a VLM, and fuses per-view predictions into a single 3D bounding box via lifting.

Our approach achieves state-of-the-art results on ScanRefer and Nr3D, and demonstrates strong generalization to unseen 3D datasets and text rephrasings.

The PanoGrounder Framework

We assume the 3D scene is given as a renderable representation (e.g., a triangle mesh or 3D Gaussian Splatting). Given such a representation and a text query, our goal is to predict a 3D bounding box of the referred object. Our key idea is to use panoramic renderings as an intermediate representation that bridges 2D vision-language understanding and 3D spatial reasoning. The pipeline operates in three stages.

1. Structure-Aware Camera Placement

Panoramic cameras capture an omnidirectional view of the scene, so we only need to select locations. We estimate the floor via RANSAC and place a regular grid over the scene's 2D floor footprint. Each candidate location is scored by three factors — ray coverage, distance-to-surface, and distance-to-trajectory — and a compact set of viewpoints is greedily selected until the scene is sufficiently covered (on average only 2.4 cameras per scene).

2. Multi-Modal Panoramic VLM

For each viewpoint we render multi-modal panoramas — RGB, geometric (range), and semantic feature maps. Geometric and semantic features are injected into selected ViT layers of the VLM through a lightweight multi-modal feature adapter (a 2-layer MLP followed by a zero-initialized 1×1 convolution), so the VLM starts from its pretrained behavior and learns task-relevant cues without distorting its representation space. The VLM then predicts a 2D bounding box on each panorama from the text query.

3. Visibility-Aware 3D Aggregation

Per-view 2D predictions are lifted into metric 3D space using the rendered depth and camera extrinsics. We select the most reliable anchor view via a cross-view consistency score, fuse the per-view point sets, filter by visibility, and fit an axis-aligned 3D bounding box to localize the referred object. The model is trained with cross-entropy combined with an Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) loss for distance-aware supervision.

Results

3D Visual Grounding on ScanRefer and Nr3D

PanoGrounder achieves state-of-the-art performance among single-dataset methods, reaching 61.0 Overall Acc@0.25 on ScanRefer and 74.6 Overall on Nr3D, with especially strong gains in multi-object scenes. Joint training on ScanRefer + ReferIt3D (S+R) further improves these to 62.0 and 76.1.

Zero-Shot Comparison with Larger VLMs

Without any fine-tuning, our pipeline with CogVLM-17B outperforms zero-shot baselines built on much larger backbones (e.g., GPT-4V, Qwen2-VL-72B), achieving 53.2 Overall on Nr3D — surpassing VLM-Grounder's 48.0 despite a significantly smaller backbone.

Generalization to Unseen Scenes and Text

The method transfers effectively to unseen 3D datasets (ARKitScenes, 3RScan) with much smaller performance degradation than prior baselines, and remains robust under challenging text perturbations — including affordance-based queries that remove the target object's class name, where it improves over PQ3D by +15.9.
